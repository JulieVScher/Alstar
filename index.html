About us

ABA in the GTA Autism Behaviour Therapy is an approved provider of the Ontario Autism Program (OAP) specializing in ABA Therapy for Teenagers and Young Adults throughout the Greater Toronto Area (GTA).

Owned and operated by a Board Certified Behaviour Analyst® (BCBA®).

ABA Therapy for Teens

Born Out of Necessity. ABA in the GTA Autism Behaviour Therapy fills the Need for ABA Services for Older Youth across the GTA.

Typically, ABA therapy is provided to young children between he ages of 2-10 in home, school, or clinic settings. However, this does not mean that ABA therapy is not effective for teens or adults. Obtaining funding or finding providers for ABA services can be challenging for older clients, and access to services may decline as the child grows. This issue can be particularly significant in rural areas or regions with limited service providers. Even in school systems that provide ABA services, the intensity and quality of ABA programs may decrease for older students. However, ABA is a broad science that includes a range of methodologies, teaching styles, techniques, and strategies. Implementing positive reinforcement, antecendent-based intervantions, or modeling can all be considered as ABA therapy. Age should not be a determining factor in accessing ABA services, and quality ABA therapy can yield impressive results for individuals of all ages and abilities.

Services

Social and Leisure Skills

Individuals on the autism spectrum typically desire interaction with others, but may need some extra support in learning how to navigate different social situations. With practice and guidance, they can develop important social skills that can help them feel more confident and comfortable engaging with their peers. Building these skills can lead to more opportunities for happiness and meaningful friendships within the community.






Executive Functioning Skills

Individuals with autism may experience challenges with executive functioning, including difficulty with planning, organization, sequencing information, and regulating emotions. Some individuals may have difficulty seeing how small details fit into a bigger picture, while others struggle with maintaining attention in different settings. Planning tasks may be difficult for some individuals, as they struggle to organize their thoughts and actions and figure out the sequence of steps needed. Complex thinking that involves holding more than one train of thought simultaneously may also be challenging for some individuals.






Daily Living Skills

Individuals with autism can sometimes require specific, incremental, and practical guidance to develop the necessary skills to live independently. This guidance should cover essential life skills such as money management, cooking, shopping, room organization, and transportation. The training should be conducted in natural environments, where the individual will live, to ensure that the skills being taught are relevant to the environment.






Self-Regulation Skills

To actively participate in community life, individuals with autism should possess the crucial skill of self-regulation. A significant number of adolescents with autism may struggle to manage their reactions effectively. This can pose a significant obstacle to social engagement. It is vital to provide them with the necessary support and tools to learn how to regulate their emotions and sensory input. This can help them better navigate various social scenarios and interact with others positively.






Caregiver and Parent Coaching

Parent and caregiver coaching is a therapeutic approach designed to help caregivers better understand and address their child's unique behavioural challenges. It involves teaching caregivers how to use positive reinforcement, and other ABA techniques, to encourage desired behaviors and discourage undesired behaviors in their child. During parent training, a therapist will work with parents to identify specific goal for intervention and use evidence-based strategies to address them. The goal of parent training is to help caregivers become more confident and effective in managing their child's behavior, which can improve family dynamics and quality of life for both the child and the parents.






Behavioural Consultation

The goal of behavioral consultation is to help individuals with behavioural, social, or communication challenges to thrive across environments. We provide behavioral consultation across recreation, school, and camp settings. Consultation might involve identifying specific behaviors that are interfering with participating in activities, developing strategies to address those behaviors, and providing training to support staff to help them implement those strategies effectively. Our goal is to help individuals to succeed and thrive by providing tailored solutions that are effective and sustainable over the long term. By working collaboratively with caregivers, educators, and other stakeholders, we can create a supportive and inclusive environment where everyone can thrive.






1:1 Home-Based and Community ABA Services

Conducting therapy in the natural environment enables a BCBA to observe behavior and identify immediate areas of need. Our clinical supervisor is responsible for conducting assessments, identifying goals and objectives, designing interventions, and providing ongoing progress monitoring. Our BCBA will regularly visit clients to ensure their personalized autism therapy plan is effective. We provide ongoing supervision of behavior therapists working directly with your child to make sure that therapy programs are being implemented with accuracy.






BCBA® and RBT® Trainee Supervision

ABA in the GTA provides affordable and ethical supervision to trainees accruing fieldwork hours towards fulfilling eligibility requirements for Board Certified Behavior Analyst® (BCBA ®) or Board Certified Assistant Behavior Analyst® (BCaBA ®) certification with the Behavior Analyst Certification Board® (BACB ®).
